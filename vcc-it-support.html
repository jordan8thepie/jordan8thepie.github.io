Initial Audit & Quick Wins

Day One: Removing the Bottleneck

Identified a legacy 100Mbps Fast Ethernet switch acting as the primary backbone. Replacing this with Gigabit hardware on day one immediately restored network throughput for the entire office fleet.

Unified Communications (VoIP) Transformation

Solving the "Shared Extension" Conflict

The Problem: Staff shared extensions with reception, meaning reception could not transfer calls to the Accounts office without the system failing due to identity collisions. Staff were also frustrated by failing cordless phone batteries.

  • 1:1 Provisioning: Migrated to a Cloud PBX with unlimited extensions, giving every desk a unique digital identity.
  • Auto Attendant & Call Queues: Enabled caller self-selection (IVR) and implemented a fail-safe call queue to ensure no call went unanswered if an option wasn't selected.
  • Mobility: Replaced all legacy cordless phones with Headsets and Softphone apps, allowing staff to work seamlessly from their laptops without battery anxiety.

Networking & Data Governance

Why I Standardized on UniFi

Upgraded a partial setup to a full UniFi SDN environment managed via a UDM-SE. I chose this ecosystem to provide a "single umbrella" for management while maintaining zero ongoing subscription costs, protecting the Trust's budget.

Identity & SharePoint Migration

Migrated fragmented local accounts to Entra ID and transitioned organizational data from personal work OneDrives to a structured SharePoint Library with role-based access controls.
