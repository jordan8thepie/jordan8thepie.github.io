Infrastructure & Systems Transformation

Victory Christian Centre Trust | Systems Administrator & AV Lead

Network Backbone & TCO Strategy

The Bottleneck: Identified a legacy 100Mbps Fast Ethernet switch on Day One that was throttling the entire office.

I led the transition to a modern UniFi SDN ecosystem, choosing to stay with UniFi to maintain a "Single Umbrella" management style while ensuring zero ongoing subscription costs. This protected the Trust from the "Subscription Creep" typical of other enterprise vendors.

  • Hardware: Deployed the UDM-SE as the core gateway.
  • Physical Layer: Repaired and livened structured cabling across multiple buildings, terminating new 24-port patch panels.
  • Performance: Successfully established a 1000Mbps (Gigabit) backbone, increasing network speed by 10x.

Cloud VoIP & Communication Logic

The Failure: "Extension Sharing" meant Reception could not call or transfer to Accounts. Cordless phones were failing mid-call due to dead batteries, causing major staff frustration.

I re-architected the telephony system to move the organization from a reactive "switchboard" model to a professional enterprise environment.

  • 1:1 Extensions: Assigned every staff member a unique digital identity, ending all transfer collisions.
  • Auto Attendant (IVR): Designed a self-service menu (e.g., "Press 1 for Accounts") allowing callers to route themselves.
  • Fail-Safe Queues: Programmed a "Blast Ring" queue to ensure all office phones ring if no menu option is selected.
  • Modern Endpoints: Decommissioned all legacy cordless phones; transitioned staff to softphones and high-quality headsets for total laptop mobility.
  • Value Engineering: Secured unlimited extensions for a marginal cost of only $5/month extra.

Identity & Data Governance

Consolidated fragmented local accounts and personal work OneDrives into a secure, governed cloud environment.

  • Entra ID: Implemented centralized identity management and MFA.
  • SharePoint Library: Built a structured data repository with Role-Based Access Controls (RBAC) to replace unmanaged file sharing.
