Day One: The Physical Audit

Upon arrival, I identified a literal "handbrake" on the Trust's productivity: a legacy 100Mbps Fast Ethernet switch acting as the primary backbone for a major section of the campus.

By replacing this bottleneck with Gigabit-capable hardware immediately, I restored 10x the network throughput for staff, establishing a baseline for the larger digital transformation to follow.

Network Consolidation

I inherited a fragmented, partially implemented UniFi setup. I led the effort to unify the entire campus under a Single Pane of Glass management system.

Strategic Hardware Choice

Deployed the UniFi Dream Machine SE. I chose to stay with the UniFi ecosystem specifically to avoid Subscription Creep—securing enterprise features with zero ongoing licensing fees.

Physical Layer Mastery

Personally repaired and "livened" structured cabling in secondary buildings, terminating a new 24-port patch panel to unify the campus backbone.

Unified Communications Overhaul

Legacy Conflict: Shared extensions meant the Receptionist could not call or transfer to the Accounts Office (and vice versa), creating circular failure loops and staff frustration.
The Resolution: 1:1 Provisioning & Automation

I migrated the Trust to a Cloud VoIP provider, negotiating unlimited extensions for a marginal $5/mo increase.

  • Auto-Attendant & IVR: Enabled caller self-selection, allowing the public to bypass the "Receptionist Bottleneck" and reach departments directly.
  • Fail-Safe Call Queues: Programmed a backup "Blast Ring" queue so if no option is selected, every office phone rings, ensuring zero abandoned calls.
  • Modern Endpoints: Decommissioned all legacy cordless phones (which suffered from battery failure and interference) and transitioned staff to softphones and headsets for total mobility.
